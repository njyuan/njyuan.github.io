<p class="p6"><span class="s1">Biography<span class="Apple-converted-space"> </span></span></p>
<p class="p7"><span class="s1">Dr. Nicholas (Jing) Yuan is currently Technical Advisor to the Corporate Vice President (CVP) at Microsoft, working on strategy, long range planning, and technical/product innovations. He also serves as Adjunct Professor in The Hong Kong University of Science and Technology. He was previously Vice President of AI, Chief Scientist and Director of Language &amp; Speech Innovation Lab at Huawei Cloud, leading a global team to develop and apply cutting-edge AI technologies empowering enterprises and governments. Before that, he was Deputy GM of AI Creation Business Team, also a Senior Applied Scientist Lead/Manager in Microsoft. Earlier than that, he worked as a researcher in Microsoft Research. He got a Ph.D degree in Computer Science and B.S. degree in Mathematics both from University of Science and Technology of China.</span></p>
<p class="p7"><span class="s4"><br>
</span><span class="s1">During the past years, Nicholas has published more than 100 papers in top-tier conferences and journals, including ACM SIGKDD, IEEE TKDE, ACM Ubicomp, ACL, ACM TOIS, WWW, ICDE, AAAI, etc. His research work has been featured by influential media such as MIT Technology Review multiple times. He served as program chair of HuMoComp 2013-2015, industry chair of DASFAA 2017, ASONAM 2016, area chair of NLPCC 2016, and regularly as program committee members in top conferences such as SIGKDD, WWW, ACL, EMNLP, AAAI, IJCAI, ICDM, and WSDM. He is a senior member of ACM, IEEE, and CCF. In 2021, he was recognized as Most Influential Scholar in data mining domain by Aminer AI 2000, ranked Top 20 worldwide and Top 2 in China. </span></p>
<p class="p7"><span class="s4"><br>
</span><span class="s1"> In Huawei Cloud, Nicholas led a multidiscipline team with a mission to accelerate intelligent transformations for various industries, such as AI landing cases on smart cities, healthcare, energy, and manufacturing. Within less than 3 years, his team grew the market share of AI cloud services from almost zero to top 3 in China. Nicholas led the AI Creation Business Team in Microsoft to develop AI technologies &amp; systems for Microsoft Xiaoice such as multi-modal modern Chinese poem generator (image to poem), music generation (Xiaoice Band), Artistic photo generation from music (music to image), and AI financial content generation platform. These projects have been featured by more than 1800 international &amp; domestic medias, including Business Insider, The Verge, South China Morning Post, CCTV (the state television broadcaster in China) and People's Daily. He also led Bing CJK Applied Science Team to develop core technologies &amp; products for Bing in China, Japan, and Korea (CJK) markets, including Bing Search App, Bing Dictionary, and pipelines for knowledge graph construction.</span><span class="s5"> </span></p>
<p class="p7"><span class="s4"><br>
</span><span class="s1">Nicholas has been honored with Microsoft Fellowship (2011), Best Application Paper Award of ACM SIGKDD (2016), Best Student Paper Award of ACM SIGKDD (2018) (as the supervisor of the first author), Best Paper Award of IEEE International Conference on Data Mining (2013), Best Paper Runner-up Award of ACM SIGSPATIAL (2010), and Distinguished Doctoral Dissertation Award of Chinese Academy of Sciences (2013). He has been honored with First-class Prize of Natural Science Award from Ministry of Education in China (2018). In particular, his pioneering work on trajectory mining received 10-Year Impact Honorable Award in ACM SIGSPATIAL 2020. He is a senior member of ACM, IEEE, and CCF.</span></p>
